This paper proposes a method for structuring accident case documents as hierarchical knowledge graphs. The approach represents diverse descriptions in a document from the perspective of predicates and entities, allowing events, causes, processes, and countermeasures that appear across multiple sections to be organized consistently. The hierarchical graph design preserves both horizontal relations among events and vertical document structure such as categories, chapters, and sections. Experiments using the Failure Knowledge Database confirmed the usefulness of the generated knowledge graphs and discussed their potential application to automated support for complex technical troubleshooting.
