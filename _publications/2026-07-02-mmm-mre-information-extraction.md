This paper investigates whether the Mutual Reinforcement Effect (MRE), where word-level and sentence-level information extraction tasks improve each other through joint modeling, generalizes beyond the Japanese settings in which it had previously been reported. The paper introduces the Multilingual MRE Mix dataset (MMM), covering English, Japanese, and Chinese, and proposes an LLM-assisted translation and alignment framework to reduce annotation effort while preserving the structural requirements of MRE tasks. Experiments with a unified input-output information extraction framework show that most MMM sub-datasets consistently exhibit MRE, providing empirical evidence for the value of multilingual MRE-based information extraction.
